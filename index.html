Where and when was treaty 1 signed?

Treaty 1 was signed in 1871 on August 3rd and it was signed in lower Fort Garry

This treaty is important because it recognizes the rights and lands of Indigenous peoples in Canada.

Where exactly?

It was signed in southern Manitoba where Winnipeg is located.

What communities are involved?

  • Anishinaabe
  • Muskegon Cree peoples
  • The Crown

Agreements

These were the agreements between the Indigenous peoples and the Crown:

  • The tribes gave up their lands within specified boundaries to the Queen.
  • Beginning at the south line of St. Peter's Parish, the Crown will give 160 acres for each family of five, or in that proportion for larger or smaller families, so if there is a family of 4, they will get 128 acres for example and the proportions will continue according to the amount of people in the family.
  • At some period in July, the tribes will be receiving a payment of 15 Canadian dollars in goods such as blankets, clothing, prints (assorted colours), twine or traps or if permitted by the Crown can be given in cash. This payment will be received by family every month in July according to the proportion of people per family. A family of 5 will receive 15 Canadian dollars and for example, if it is a family of 4, the payment will be 12 Canadian dollars, however each chief will get an additional payment of 20 Canadian dollars in goods or cash and an additional suit of clothing to the chief every three years.
  • At the treaty's signing, every tribe member gets a gift worth 3 Canadian dollars.
  • If the reserve desires, the Queen permits a school to be maintained on the reserve.
  • Alcohol and intoxicating liquor is not permitted to be sold or introduced on reserves.
  • The Queen and the tribes promised peace between the settlers and the tribes.

How is treaty 1 different from other treatys?

  • The treaty was among the first of the treaties signed by the Indigenous people.
  • Treaty 1 had very specific geographic focuses on where it will be located unlike the other treaties.
  • The prohibition of alcohol was rarely signed on treaties, but on Treaty 1, it did.
